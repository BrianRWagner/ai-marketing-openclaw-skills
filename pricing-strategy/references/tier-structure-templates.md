# Tier Structure Templates

## How Many Tiers?

**2 tiers:** Simple, clear choice — works for clear SMB vs. Enterprise split
**3 tiers:** Industry standard — Good/Better/Best
**4+ tiers:** Wide range of customer sizes — risk of decision paralysis

## Good-Better-Best Framework

**Good (Entry):** Remove barriers. Core features, limited usage. Small teams.
**Better (Recommended):** Where most customers land. Full features, reasonable limits. Anchor price.
**Best (Premium):** Capture high-value customers. Everything + advanced features. 2-3x Better pricing.

## Tier Differentiation Strategies

**Feature gating:** Basic features in all tiers, advanced in higher tiers
**Usage limits:** Same features, different limits (users, storage, API calls)
**Support level:** Email → Priority → Dedicated success
**Access/customization:** API, SSO, custom branding for enterprise

## Pricing Checklist

### Before Setting Prices
- [ ] Defined target customer personas
- [ ] Researched competitor pricing
- [ ] Identified value metric
- [ ] Conducted WTP research
- [ ] Mapped features to tiers

### Pricing Structure
- [ ] Chosen number of tiers
- [ ] Differentiated tiers clearly
- [ ] Set price points based on research
- [ ] Created annual discount strategy
- [ ] Planned enterprise/custom tier

### Validation
- [ ] Tested pricing with target customers
- [ ] Reviewed with sales team
- [ ] Validated unit economics
- [ ] Planned for price increases
- [ ] Set up tracking for pricing metrics

## Pricing Page Psychology

- **Anchoring:** Show higher-priced option first
- **Decoy effect:** Middle tier should be obviously best value
- **Charm pricing:** $49 vs. $50 (value-focused)
- **Round pricing:** $50 vs. $49 (premium)
- **Annual savings:** Show monthly price but offer annual discount (17-20%)
