# Email Timing & Copy Guidelines

## Timing/Delays by Sequence Type

| Sequence | Recommended Timing |
|----------|-------------------|
| Welcome | Immediately |
| Early sequence | 1-2 days apart |
| Lead nurture | 2-4 days apart |
| Long-term nurture | Weekly or bi-weekly |

B2B: Avoid weekends | B2C: Test weekends | Time zones: Send at local time

## Sequence Length Reference

| Type | Emails | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| Welcome | 3-7 | 14 days |
| Lead nurture | 5-10 | 21 days |
| Onboarding | 5-10 | 14 days |
| Re-engagement | 3-5 | 14 days |

Depends on sales cycle length, product complexity, relationship stage.

## Subject Line Strategy

- Clear > Clever
- Specific > Vague
- Benefit or curiosity-driven
- 40-60 characters ideal

**Patterns:**
- Question: "Still struggling with X?"
- How-to: "How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]"
- Number: "3 ways to [benefit]"
- Direct: "[First name], your [thing] is ready"
- Story tease: "The mistake I made with [topic]"

## Email Copy Guidelines

### Structure
1. Hook: First line grabs attention
2. Context: Why this matters to them
3. Value: The useful content
4. CTA: What to do next
5. Sign-off: Human, warm close

### Length
- 50-125 words: Transactional
- 150-300 words: Educational
- 300-500 words: Story-driven

### Formatting
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- White space between sections
- Bold for emphasis (sparingly)
- Mobile-first — most read on phone

## Personalization

**Merge fields:** First name (fallback: "there"), Company name, Relevant data

**Segmentation:**
- By behavior: Openers vs. non-openers, Clickers vs. non-clickers
- By stage: Trial vs. paid, New vs. long-term
- By profile: Industry/role, Use case, Company size

## Metrics Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Open rate | 20-40% |
| Click rate | 2-5% |
| Unsubscribe rate | Keep under 0.5% |
